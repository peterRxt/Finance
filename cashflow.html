Comprehensive Guide: Cash Flow & Balance Sheet (Kenyan Hardware Business)

1. Understanding a Cash Flow Statement

A Cash Flow Statement helps you monitor the actual money flowing in and out of your business monthly. Unlike profit, it shows real-time liquidity and ability to pay bills.

Sections of a Cash Flow

  • Cash Inflows: Sales, loan disbursements, customer debt repayments, asset sales.
  • Cash Outflows: Inventory purchases, salaries, bills, tax, rent, loan repayments, statutory payments.

Formulas:
Net Cash Flow = Total Inflows - Total Outflows
Closing Balance = Opening Balance + Net Cash Flow

2. Understanding a Balance Sheet

A Balance Sheet shows your store's financial position: what you own, what you owe, and your equity.

Basic Equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity

Assets

  • Current Assets: Cash, Inventory, Accounts Receivable, Prepaid Rent
  • Fixed Assets: Land, Building, Computers, Vehicles

Liabilities

  • Current Liabilities: Supplier bills, VAT/NHIF/PAYE due, Salaries payable
  • Non-Current Liabilities: Long-term loans

Equity

  • Owner's Contribution (Capital)
  • Retained Earnings (Net profit carried forward)
